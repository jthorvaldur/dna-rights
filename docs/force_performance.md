# Force Performance

Demanding the court perform on biological rights. If the court claims authority over the parent-offspring bond, make the court prove it.

## Specific Performance in Equity

In contract law, "specific performance" is a remedy that compels a party to fulfill their obligation — not just pay damages, but actually perform. The same principle applies to the court itself:

- The court has an obligation to operate within its jurisdiction
- The court has an obligation to identify the source of its authority when challenged
- The court has an obligation to recognize rights it did not create and cannot revoke

When the court orders separation of a parent from their offspring, you can demand: **"Under what authority do you sever a biological bond?"**

## The Pattern

### Step 1: Demand

Present the biological fact (DNA parentage) and demand the court acknowledge it:

> "Respondent demands that this Court acknowledge the biological parentage of [offspring], as established by DNA, and identify the specific authority under which this Court claims jurisdiction to modify or sever that biological bond."

### Step 2: Court Response

The court must respond in one of three ways:

1. **Acknowledge** that it lacks authority over the biological bond (which undermines custody orders that sever the bond)
2. **Identify** the specific statutory authority it relies on (which can then be challenged as insufficient to override a natural right)
3. **Ignore** the demand (which creates an appealable record — failure to address a jurisdictional challenge)

Every response shifts the frame.

### Step 3: Jurisdictional Challenge

Whatever the court responds, follow up with a jurisdictional challenge:

- If the court acknowledges it lacks biological jurisdiction: "Then this Court's order separating Respondent from their offspring exceeds the Court's jurisdiction."
- If the court cites statutory authority: "Statutory authority cannot override a natural right. The parent-offspring bond predates and supersedes 750 ILCS 5/."
- If the court ignores the demand: "Respondent notes for the record that this Court has failed to identify its authority to sever a biological bond, which constitutes a jurisdictional defect."

### Step 4: Escalation

If the trial court fails to address the jurisdictional challenge, it becomes grounds for appellate review:

- Jurisdictional questions can be raised at any time, including on appeal
- Failure to establish jurisdiction is not waivable
- An order issued without jurisdiction is void, not merely voidable

## Connection to the Sovereign Framework

The force performance pattern aligns with the questioning framework from sovereign-legal:

| Sovereign Question | DNA Rights Application |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| "Under what authority?" | "Under what authority does this Court sever a biological bond?" |
| "Show me the contract." | "Show me the instrument that grants jurisdiction over DNA." |
| "Who is the injured party?" | "Whose natural rights are being violated by this separation?" |
| "What is the consideration?" | "What does the parent receive in exchange for severance of the natural bond?" |

## Practical Application

### In a Motion

> "COMES NOW Respondent and demands that this Court specifically perform its obligation to identify the jurisdictional basis for any order that separates Respondent from his/her biological offspring.
>
> Respondent is the biological parent of [names], as established by DNA. The parent-offspring bond is a natural right that exists independent of any legal proceeding. (*Troxel v. Granville*, 530 U.S. 57 (2000); *Stanley v. Illinois*, 405 U.S. 645 (1972)).
>
> Before this Court issues any order affecting the biological parent-offspring bond, Respondent demands that the Court:
>
> 1. Identify the specific authority under which it claims jurisdiction over a biological fact;
> 2. Identify the legal standard by which a natural right may be overridden by judicial order;
> 3. Identify the compelling state interest that justifies interference with the most fundamental of natural bonds."

### In Response to a Custody Order

> "This order purports to allocate 'parenting time' as if the parent-offspring bond is a commodity subject to judicial distribution. Respondent demands that the Court identify its authority to treat a biological bond as divisible property subject to allocation."

## The Core Principle

The court's legitimacy depends on acting within its jurisdiction. When you demand performance — demand the court identify its authority — you are not being disrespectful. You are exercising the fundamental right of every person subject to a court's power: the right to challenge that power's basis.

A court that cannot identify its authority to act is a court acting without authority.
